Over the course of my MSci and PhD, I have gathered a lot of esoteric knowledge which I want to gather into one place and try to make more digestible. I've decided to include less obscure knowledge too, to try making these pages self-contained.

Goals for this website:

  • Be very lightweight. Nobody likes autoplaying animations that slow your machine to a crawl
  • Work on every device and browser. I'm not a fan of the Firefox/Chromium duopoly, sadly the vast majority of websites don't work with any other option. I want this website to work on alternate browsers like Dillo and Lynx. I will be using these two browsers as test cases for functionality. I hope it works with Ladybird too but that's not in my repos.
  • Be accessible to a wide audience to the extent possible. I feel like some topics are actually much easier than they're made out to be.
  • Contain everything I know that might be useful to someone.
As a result of the above goals, I will be experimenting with TeXicode, which renders mathematics written in LaTeX in unicode. This should have wide support on alternate browsers that don't support MathML. I will also try having a PDF version available of heavily mathematical pages, to not compromise on quality. Here's an example: 
 ⌠¹     ⎡ x⁴ ⎤¹  1 
 │ x³dx=⎢╶──╴⎥ =╶─╴
 ⌡₀     ⎣ 4  ⎦₀  4
Looks pretty good for pure unicode, and I've confirmed it's legible on both Lynx and Dillo. On Lynx I had to enable a locale-based unicode set to get a reasonable output. I will be including PNG figures when graphs are necessary. I wanted SVGs but dillo's SVG support sucks.
